DE ZoomCamp 2026


🎯 Problem Statement

Climate change is widely recognized as one of the most pressing global challenges, with rising global temperatures often linked to increasing carbon emissions from human activities.

However, understanding the relationship between carbon emissions and temperature change is not straightforward. While datasets provide detailed records of CO₂ emissions and temperature changes, they are often stored separately and are not structured for combined analysis.

This project addresses a fundamental question:

To what extent do increases in CO₂ emissions contribute to rising global temperatures over time?

To answer this, we combine country-level emissions data and temperature data into a unified analytical pipeline, enabling integrated analysis across time and geography.

The project introduces a key metric:

Climate Impact Score
Climate Impact Score = CO₂ Growth Rate × Temperature Growth Rate

A higher score indicates stronger alignment between emission growth and temperature increase
A lower score suggests weaker or delayed impact

Through an end-to-end data pipeline, this project transforms raw environmental datasets into a structured analytical model and interactive dashboard that allows users to:

Track CO₂ emissions and temperature trends over time
Analyze correlations between emissions and temperature changes
Compare environmental impact across countries
Identify patterns that may support climate policy discussions

By bridging the gap between emissions data and climate outcomes, this project demonstrates how data engineering can support deeper understanding of global climate dynamics.
